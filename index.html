Smart Energy Monitor

Embedded C, Electronics and PCB Design

I collaborated with my team to develop analog/digital hardware for monitoring and controlling energy usage, designing circuits on paper and testing them with LTSpice. We used an ATmega328p IC for the control system and developed firmware with Proteus and Microchip Studio, followed by verification, validation, and PCB design in Altium using SMT parts. Finally, I established a communication channel between the hardware and the computer to smartly control loads.

Water Tank Level Monitor

Electronics, Analogue Design

Design and implementation of an electronic system for assessing water levels in a tank, following the specifications of our client. The system comprises a vertical capacitive probe, a transmitter circuit, and a receiver circuit, crafted to optimize range, stability, and accuracy. Using engineering and analysis skills, key design choices were made to ensure high quality.

Auckland Programme for Space Systems

Safety Critical Embeded Systems and Space grade electronics

Launched in 2016, the Auckland Programme for Space Systems (APSS) is designed for undergraduate students of all fields of study at the University of Auckland to collaborate towards reach a common goal – contributing broadly towards the field of space research. Each year, second and third year undergraduate students are invited to form teams, work together to identify a societal need, and design a solution using a CubeSat. Upon completion, the satellite will be launched into space by an orbital provider.
